# Research: The Time Tax of Glasses & Contact Lenses

## Core Concept
People with poor vision pay a hidden "time tax" every day — minutes spent on routines, maintenance, adjustments, and workarounds that people with good (or corrected-to-free) vision never think about. Over a lifetime, this adds up to weeks and months of lost time.

---

## 1. Contact Lens Daily Time Tax

### The Daily Routine (measurable, per CDC/AOA guidelines):
- **Hand washing** before insertion: 1 minute (CDC recommendation: 20-30 sec scrubbing + drying)
- **Lens inspection & insertion** (both eyes): 2-5 minutes (beginners 5+, experienced 2-3 min)
- **Hand washing** before removal: 1 minute
- **Lens removal** (both eyes): 1-2 minutes
- **Cleaning & rubbing** each lens (multipurpose solution): 20-30 sec per side × 2 lenses = ~2 min
- **Rinsing** lenses: 5-10 sec per side × 2 lenses = ~40 sec
- **Case cleaning, filling with solution**: 1-2 min
- **Buying supplies** (solution, cases, lenses): trips to pharmacy/ordering — estimated 15-30 min/month

### Conservative Daily Total: 8-12 minutes/day
- Source: AOA lens care guidelines (aoa.org), CDC contact lens care recommendations

### Annual Calculation:
- **8 min/day × 365 = 2,920 min = ~49 hours/year** (conservative)
- **12 min/day × 365 = 4,380 min = ~73 hours/year** (thorough routine)
- **Add monthly supply runs: ~4-6 hours/year**
- **Add yearly eye exam + fitting: ~3 hours (including travel)**

### **TOTAL: 55-80 hours per year for contact lens wearers**
### **Over 30 years of lens wear: 1,650-2,400 hours = 69-100 FULL DAYS of life**

### Additional time costs for contacts:
- Eye drops / rewetting drops during day: 2-5 min/day for dry eye sufferers (70% of wearers report end-of-day discomfort)
- Emergency situations (lost lens, torn lens): ~30 min per incident, avg 3-6 times/year
- Source: 70% discomfort stat from previous research; CDC reports 99% of CL wearers engage in at least one risky behavior

---

## 2. Glasses Daily Time Tax

### The Daily Routine:
- **Morning search / locating glasses**: 1-3 minutes (glasses are 2nd-4th most commonly lost item in multiple surveys)
- **Cleaning lenses** (fogging, smudging): 2-5 minutes/day across multiple cleaning episodes
  - 86% of glasses wearers experience fogging issues (previous research)
  - 1 in 4 clean glasses daily (NVISION survey, 2024)
  - Average person cleans/wipes glasses 3-6 times per day
- **Adjusting frames** on nose/ears: micro-moments throughout day, est. 1-2 min cumulative
- **Switching glasses** (reading, distance, sun): 2-5 min/day for multi-pair users
- **Shopping for new glasses** (every 1-2 years): 2-4 hours per shopping trip
- **Eye exams** for prescription updates: 2-3 hours/year including travel
- **Repair / adjustment visits** to optician: 1-2 hours/year (2x per year recommended)

### Conservative Daily Total: 5-10 minutes/day
### Annual Calculation:
- **5 min/day × 365 = 1,825 min = ~30 hours/year** (minimal)
- **10 min/day × 365 = 3,650 min = ~61 hours/year** (active lifestyle/heavy use)
- **Add shopping, exams, repairs: ~8-12 hours/year**

### **TOTAL: 38-73 hours per year for glasses wearers**
### **Over 40 years: 1,520-2,920 hours = 63-122 FULL DAYS of life**

## 3. Lost Item Data (Glasses Specifically)

- **Glasses/sunglasses are the #2 most commonly lost item** in UK (Samsung/The Sun, Jan 2026) and #4 in US (Express.co.uk study of 2,000 adults)
- **Americans lose avg 5 items per month**, spend **nearly 17 hours/year** searching for lost items, ~16 min per item (Shane Co. / Tile survey, via AOL)
- **27% of Americans misplace their glasses at least once per week** (Pixie "Lost & Found" survey, Apartment Therapy)
- **Average search for misplaced item: 5 min 20 sec** (Pixie survey)
- **Brits misplace at least 3 physical items per month**, dedicate up to 1 hour searching (Samsung, 2026)
- **Brits lose avg 4 items/month** — glasses/sunglasses is #4 most common (Express.co.uk, study of 2,000)
- Over a lifetime, people spend **~2.5 days per year** searching for all lost items (Pixie)

## 4. Productivity & Work Impact

- **Vision impairment costs $410.7 billion globally** in lost productivity annually (EClinicalMedicine, 2021, PMC)
- **Workers experience eye strain after just 27 minutes** of screen work (VisionMonday, 2024 survey of 2,000)
- **83.6% of Americans ignore the 20-20-20 rule** (NVISION survey, 2024)
- **Glasses-related productivity disruptions**: fogging in meetings, adjusting during presentations, switching reading/distance glasses, cleaning before detailed work
- Refractive surgery shown as **most cost-saving technique** compared to spectacles and contacts (Frontiers/PMC, 2018 Iranian study)

## 5. The "Invisible" Time Costs (qualitative)

Things that never show up in studies but every glasses/contacts wearer knows:

### Morning Penalty:
- Can't see alarm clock without glasses
- First task of every day: find and put on glasses / insert lenses
- People with good vision: open eyes → ready

### Sports & Activity Tax:
- Changing to sports glasses/goggles: 2-5 min
- Removing contacts before swimming: 3-5 min
- Dealing with foggy glasses at gym: repeated interruptions
- Ski goggles + glasses compatibility issues: time spent adjusting

### Travel Tax:
- Packing contact lens supplies, backup glasses, solutions
- Airport security: removing/replacing glasses
- Carrying lens case, solution bottles (liquid restrictions)
- Finding pharmacy abroad when supplies run out

### Weather Tax:
- Wiping rain off lenses
- Fogging when entering warm buildings from cold
- Fogging from masks (86% reported this)
- Condensation in humid climates

### Social/Aesthetic Tax:
- Time spent choosing frames that "look right"
- Self-consciousness and identity concerns
- 51% of wearers report physical discomfort on nose/ears

### Safety Tax:
- Night driving with dirty/scratched lenses — slower, more cautious
- Sports without correction — hesitation, reduced performance
- Emergency situations — can't see without glasses
