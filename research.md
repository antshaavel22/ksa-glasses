## 1. Pain Points of Wearing Glasses

### Key Statistics

- **75%** of American adults need some form of vision correction; **64%** wear prescription eyeglasses — roughly 197.6 million people. ([Glasson Optical Software, 2025](https://www.glasson.app/blog/what-percentage-of-people-wear-glasses-the-global-vision-correction-reality/))
- **59%** of UK adults without perfect vision are *regularly frustrated* by their sight; **23%** say it has impacted their confidence. ([Association of Optometrists, 2024](https://www.aop.org.uk/ot/news/2024/12/12/survey-highlights-levels-of-frustration-caused-by-imperfect-vision))
- **18%** of glasses wearers report they lack confidence in themselves when wearing glasses. ([AOP / J&J Survey, 2024](https://www.aop.org.uk/ot/news/2024/12/12/survey-highlights-levels-of-frustration-caused-by-imperfect-vision))
- **51.3%** of glasses wearers complain that glasses hurt their nose or ears. ([Clinical Ophthalmology / PMC study, 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10680458/))
- **54.7%** of contact lens wearers AND spectacle wearers stated the main barrier to compliance is being *annoyed and bothered* by wearing them. ([PMC, 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10680458/))
- **75.2%** of glasses wearers believe long-term use of glasses will make them *permanently dependent* on eyeglasses. ([PMC, 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10680458/))
- **72.5%** of people planning laser correction cited *fear of dependence on glasses/contacts* as the primary motivation. ([PMC, 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10680458/))
- **36%** of adults admit to using their shirt or clothing to clean glasses — a practice dermatologists and eye care providers explicitly discourage. ([The Vision Council / Optical Prism, 2024](https://opticalprism.ca/new-research-reveals-more-than-one-third-of-eyewear-users-neglect-proper-care/))
- **1 in 4 (25.4%)** glasses wearers wash their glasses *once a month or less*, despite needing daily cleaning to maintain optimal vision. ([NVISION Eye Centers, 2024](https://www.nvisioncenters.com/survey-americas-bad-eye-care-habits/))
- **47.8%** of glasses-only wearers have a high interest in trying contact lenses, with "freedom from glasses" (44%) and "personal appearance" (42%) as top motivators. ([Review of Optometric Business, 2024](https://reviewob.com/new-research-findings-how-many-glasses-wearers-want-to-try-cls/))

### Mask Fogging (COVID & Beyond)
- **86%** of spectacles-only wearers experienced fogging when wearing a face mask — across a survey of 8,000+ adults in 8 countries (US, UK, Japan, Italy, Canada, Australia, Netherlands, Sweden). ([CooperVision / YouGov, 2021](https://coopervision.com/our-company/news-center/press-release/more-8-10-spectacles-wearers-experience-mask-related-fogging))
- **30%** of glasses wearers removed their spectacles entirely (risking blurred vision) to escape fogging while masked; **21%** wore their masks incorrectly (nose uncovered) for relief. ([CooperVision, 2021](https://coopervision.com/our-company/news-center/press-release/more-8-10-spectacles-wearers-experience-mask-related-fogging))
- **68.2%** of pediatricians surveyed cited fogged glasses as the #1 drawback of mask-wearing in children. ([Frontiers in Pediatrics, 2021](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pediatrics/articles/10.3389/fped.2021.676718/full))
- **27%** of parents of mask-wearing children with glasses reported visual discomfort caused by fogging. ([Frontiers in Pediatrics, 2021](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/pediatrics/articles/10.3389/fped.2021.676718/full))

### Sports & Activity Limitations
- Glasses provide no peripheral vision protection; the frame physically cuts off the field of view outside the lens boundary.
- Peripheral defocus from spectacle lenses was shown to increase stair-negotiation time by up to **29.6%** in laboratory settings. ([Investigative Ophthalmology & Visual Science, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11216271/))
- **40,000+** sports-related eye injuries occur annually in the US; regular glasses provide zero protective value in most sports. ([American Academy of Ophthalmology, cited in CoEyewear](https://coewear.com/blogs/news/beyond-the-field-the-unseen-benefits-of-sports-glasses-for-athletes))

### Self-Image
- A published study (n=517 university students) found that people without glasses were rated significantly higher on **attractiveness** and **confidence** vs. the same individuals with glasses. Attractiveness difference was ~2.2 points (out of 10) for women, ~1.5 for men. ([Cureus / PMC, 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9045786/))
- Teenagers who wear glasses were found to have lower self-esteem over time compared to those who wore contact lenses, per a longitudinal study on 12–17-year-olds. ([JSR.org, 2024](https://www.jsr.org/hs/index.php/path/article/download/7395/3412/55103))

### Dependency Feelings
- Fear of being "dependent on glasses and/or lenses" is the #1 stated reason for seeking refractive surgery (72.5% of surgical candidates). ([PMC / Clinical Ophthalmology, 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10680458/))

## 2. Pain Points of Contact Lenses

### Dry Eyes & Discomfort
- **70%** of contact lens wearers report experiencing discomfort by *end of the day*. ([PMC / Life journal, 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9605398/))
- **40%** of soft contact lens wearers report dry eye as their most common complaint; **25%** experience moderate to severe symptoms. ([PMC / Life journal, 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9605398/))
- **54.7%** of contact lens users report contact lens-associated dry eye (CLADE) in published clinical research. ([Cureus / PMC, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11584416/))
- **10%–50%** of contact lens wearers **drop out** of lens wear within 3 years due primarily to discomfort. ([PMC / Life, 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC9605398/))
- **25%** dropout rate among new wearers, with **50% of dropouts occurring within the first 60 days**. ([Eyes on Eyecare](https://eyesoneyecare.com/resources/putting-a-number-to-soft-contact-lens-comfort-with-questionnaire/))

### Infection Risk
- **~99%** of contact lens wearers engage in at least one risky hygiene behavior that increases the risk of eye infection. ([CDC MMWR, 2015](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/mm6432a2.htm))
- **1 in 3 (33%)** contact lens wearers have experienced a red or painful eye requiring a doctor visit while wearing lenses. ([CDC, 2015](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/mm6432a2.htm))
- **~1 million** US healthcare visits for keratitis (corneal inflammation) or contact lens complications occur annually — at a cost of **$175 million**. ([CDC MMWR, 2015](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/mm6432a2.htm))
- Since 2006, there have been **multiple national outbreaks** of *Acanthamoeba* keratitis and fungal keratitis in the US — both potentially blinding conditions — linked to contact lens wear. ([CDC](https://www.cdc.gov/contact-lenses/about/index.html))
- **85–88%** of adolescent and adult contact lens wearers reported at least one infection-risk behavior in 2016. ([CDC MMWR, 2017](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/volumes/66/wr/mm6632a2.htm))
  - 33% sleep or nap in lenses
  - 44% wear lenses longer than prescribed
  - 33% swim while wearing lenses

### Environmental Waste
- Americans flush **2.6–2.9 billion contact lenses** down the drain every year. ([Scientific American / Arizona State University, 2018](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/contact-lenses-are-a-surprising-source-of-pollution/))
- This results in **11,000–12,650 kilograms** (~27,000 lbs) of plastic contact lens fragments accumulating in US soil annually. ([Scientific American, 2018](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/contact-lenses-are-a-surprising-source-of-pollution/))
- Research shows contact lenses exposed to sunlight can shed **90,000+ microplastic particles per year** per lens. ([American Chemical Society, 2023](https://www.acs.org/pressroom/presspacs/2023/june/contact-lenses-shed-microplastics.html))
- An annual supply of daily disposable lenses generates **~1 kg of plastic waste** per year (cartons, blister packs, foil); monthly lenses + solutions generate ~0.87 kg. ([Contact Lens Spectrum, 2019](https://clspectrum.com/issues/2019/august/the-environmental-impact-of-contact-lens-waste/))

### Daily Hassle & Travel
- Contact lenses require daily insertion/removal, cleaning solutions, lens cases, replacement schedules — a significant time investment.
- Contacts cannot be worn while napping or sleeping (risk of serious infection).
- Swimming or showering with contacts is medically contraindicated (exposure to *Acanthamoeba* in water; **44.5%** admit to swimming in contacts anyway per NVISION survey).
- Travel requires packing solutions, extra lenses, cases — a recurring inconvenience.

## 3. Lifetime Cost of Glasses vs. Contacts vs. Laser Correction

### Glasses Cost
- Average cost of prescription glasses in the US: **$200–$300** per pair; without insurance: **~$350**. Designer frames can reach $1,000+. ([Glasses.com, 2026](https://www.glasses.com/gl-us/blog/how-much-do-glasses-cost))
- Most eye doctors recommend replacing glasses every **2–3 years**. ([Lensabl, 2025](https://www.lensabl.com/blogs/posts/how-often-should-you-replace-your-glasses))
- Many people buy multiple pairs (work, sports, sunglasses, backup), multiplying annual costs.
- Lifetime cost calculation (age 20–70, 50-year span):
  - **Low cost ($150/pair, once/year):** $7,500 over 50 years
  - **Low cost, multiple pairs:** $15,000 over 50 years
  - **High quality ($500/pair, every 2 years):** $12,500 over 50 years
  - **High quality, replaced annually:** $25,000 over 50 years
  ([Image Plus Laser Eye Centre, 2018](https://www.imageplus.ca/updates/the-lifetime-cost-of-glasses/))

### Contact Lens Cost
- Average annual contact lens cost: **$250–$750** depending on type, not including solutions. ([Campbell Cunningham Taylor & Haun, 2025](https://ccteyes.com/2025/08/27/lasik-vs-glasses-and-contacts-which-costs-more-in-the-long-run/))
  - Monthly lenses: ~$30–$50/month = **$360–$600/year** for lenses alone
  - Daily disposable lenses: up to **$660–$900+/year** for full-time wear
  - Cleaning solution & cases: **$50–$80/year** additional
- **10-year contact lens cost: $3,000–$7,500+** (lenses only; add solutions, exams) ([Campbell Cunningham Taylor & Haun, 2025](https://ccteyes.com/2025/08/27/lasik-vs-glasses-and-contacts-which-costs-more-in-the-long-run/))

### Key Takeaway
- **LASIK/PRK typically pays for itself within 4–6 years** compared to ongoing glasses/contact costs. ([Campbell Cunningham Taylor & Haun](https://ccteyes.com/2025/08/27/lasik-vs-glasses-and-contacts-which-costs-more-in-the-long-run/))
- Over 20 years, contact lens wearers (dailies) can spend **$13,000–$18,000** — 3–4x the one-time laser correction cost.

## 4. Benefits of Laser Vision Correction (TransPRK / Surface Ablation)

### Visual Outcomes
- **86–94%** of surface ablation (PRK/TransPRK) patients achieve 20/20 uncorrected vision or better after healing. ([Greenwich Eye, 2026](https://greenwicheye.com/article/prk-vs-lasek-vs-epi-lasik-surface-ablation/))
- **98% of TPRK eyes** vs. 90% of FS-LASIK eyes achieved 20/20 or better Snellen UDVA at 12 months in a direct comparison. ([BMC Ophthalmology, 2020](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7304146/))
- **87%** of TPRK eyes vs. 73% of FS-LASIK eyes achieved within ±0.50D target refraction. ([BMC Ophthalmology, 2020](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7304146/))
- Clinical outcomes of TPRK were found to be **slightly better than FS-LASIK** for high myopia correction. ([BMC Ophthalmology, 2020](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7304146/))

### No Fogging
- Laser-corrected patients experience the world with **natural, unobstructed vision** — no fog from masks, humidity, rain, steam, or cold air.

### Better Peripheral Vision
- Glasses have an inherent frame boundary; correction is only provided within the lens. With laser-corrected eyes, the entire visual field is clear — including periphery.
- Peripheral defocus induced by spectacle lenses measurably slows mobility and reaction times. ([IOVS, 2024](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11216271/))

### Career Opportunities
- The **US military** has performed refractive surgery on **350,000+ service members**, with **~95% achieving 20/20 or better**. PRK is the preferred procedure for pilots and special forces due to the absence of a flap that could be displaced in combat or high-G maneuvers. ([US Army Benefits, 2021](https://myarmybenefits.us.army.mil/News/Military-Laser-Eye-Surgery-Enhancing-Vision-Readiness))
- **Commercial pilots** can undergo laser correction (LASIK or TransPRK) and return to flying once the eyes are stable with no complications. The UK Ministry of Defence accepts post-PRK/LASIK candidates. ([Oculase, 2022](https://oculase.com/blog/can-i-have-laser-eye-surgery-as-a-pilot/))
- **NASA approved LASIK for astronauts** in September 2007. ([Refractive Surgery Council, 2025](https://americanrefractivesurgerycouncil.org/lasik-for-pilots-astronauts/))
- The US Navy provides laser correction directly to its pilots, including aircraft carrier aviators and SEAL commandos. ([Refractive Surgery Council, 2025](https://americanrefractivesurgerycouncil.org/lasik-for-pilots-astronauts/))

### Sports Freedom
- Athletes report transformation in performance post-surgery:
  - **Tiger Woods** (LASIK, 1999): went from near-legally blind to 20/15 — "My vision is fantastic." Multiple Masters wins followed. ([King LASIK](https://www.kinglasik.com/blog/athletes-whove-had-lasik-surgery/))
  - **LeBron James** (LASIK, 2007): "King James can see the rim and net clearer than ever." Won 4 MVPs after surgery.
  - **Troy Aikman** (LASIK, 1998): vision improved from 20/600 to 20/20 — "This procedure definitely has the wow factor." ([King LASIK](https://www.kinglasik.com/blog/athletes-whove-had-lasik-surgery/))
  - **Greg Maddux** (LASIK): pitched a one-hitter two days after surgery. "I see better now, I know I see a lot wider now."
  - Also: Lindsey Vonn, Dwayne Wade, Tiki Barber, Rory McIlroy, Dorothy Hamill, Patrick Ewing, Amy Van Dyken.

### Morning Routine & Travel Simplified
- Post-surgery patients wake up with immediate clear vision — no searching for glasses, no insertion/removal routine.
- Travel becomes simpler: no contact lens solutions, extra lenses, or case to pack.
- Spontaneous activities (swimming, overnight stays, camping) are no longer constrained by visual correction needs.

## 5. Safety of Modern Laser Eye Correction

### Patient Satisfaction — The Hard Numbers
- **98% satisfaction rate** for LASIK/modern laser correction — the most widely cited figure in peer-reviewed literature. ([TLC Vision, 2025](https://www.tlcvision.com/blog/lasik-success-rate/))
- **FDA PROWL Study (2016):** 98% and 96% of military and civilian LASIK patients respectively reported satisfaction with their vision. ([JCRS, 2016 / Maloney-Shamie Vision, 2025](https://www.maloneyshamievision.com/blog/new-research-lasik-safety-and-performance-continue-to-impress/))
- **Sandoval et al. JCRS 2016** meta-analysis (143 articles): Only **1.2% of 9,726 LASIK patients** were dissatisfied. 46.4% were "very satisfied." ([JCRS, 2016](https://www.mneye.com/wp-content/uploads/2017/12/Sandoval_-Modern-LASIK-Outcomes_-JCRS_-2016_.pdf))
- **92.6%** satisfaction reported in a literature review of 95 studies on LASIK by Susanna et al. (2025). ([Greenwich Eye, 2026](https://greenwicheye.com/article/prk-vs-lasek-vs-epi-lasik-surface-ablation/))
- **95.4%** of patients satisfied with visual acuity after LASIK per meta-analysis (multiple studies). ([Journal of Ophthalmology, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11730016/))
- **99%** of patients achieve 20/40 vision or better; **90%** achieve 20/20 or better; **50%** achieve better than 20/20. ([TLC Vision](https://www.tlcvision.com/blog/lasik-success-rate/))

### FDA PROWL Study Key Findings (2016)
- The FDA-sponsored PROWL (Patient-Reported Outcomes with LASIK) studies enrolled ~574 subjects (military and civilians) across 5 sites.
- The procedure was found to be **more likely to improve symptoms of dry eye, glare, halos, and starbursts than to cause them** in well-selected patients.
- "With modern equipment, modern techniques and well-selected patients, good surgeons can deliver terrific results with a high degree of safety." — Dr. Daniel Durrie, FDA PROWL investigator. ([Maloney-Shamie Vision, 2025](https://www.maloneyshamievision.com/blog/new-research-lasik-safety-and-performance-continue-to-impress/))

### Surface Ablation (PRK/TransPRK) vs. LASIK — Comparative Safety

#### Ectasia Risk
- Ectasia (progressive corneal thinning — the most feared complication) is **4.5x more likely after LASIK than after PRK/surface ablation**. ([PMC / Ophthalmology and Therapy, 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8589911/))
- Incidence data without identifiable preoperative risk factors:
  - **LASIK:** 90 per 100,000 eyes (0.09%)
  - **PRK/surface ablation:** 20 per 100,000 eyes (0.02%)
  - **SMILE:** 11 per 100,000 eyes (0.011%)
  ([PMC / Ophthalmology and Therapy, 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8589911/))
- Surface ablation eliminates the corneal flap entirely — **zero flap-related complications**. LASIK flap complication rate is reported between 0.16%–15% depending on technique. ([CRST Global](https://crstodayeurope.com/articles/2012-apr/a-review-of-lasik-flap-complications/))
- Another review found ectasia is **30x more prevalent after LASIK than surface ablation**. ([CRS Today, 2006](https://crstoday.com/articles/2006-oct/crst1006_09-php))

#### Post-Operative Dry Eye
- A prospective study of 251 eyes found **LASIK caused significantly more dry eye disease than PRK or LASEK** at 6 months post-surgery (DEWS score: LASIK 1.07 vs. PRK 0.78, p=0.01). ([Journal of Clinical Medicine / PMC, 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10253504/))
- A large study of 25,317 patients found LASIK patients had higher rates of serious dry eye (52.4%) vs. PRK patients (38%). ([Precision Vision London, 2025](https://precisionvisionlondon.com/prk-vs-lasik-london-eye-surgeons-reveal-which-is-safer/))
- PRK corneal sensitivity normalizes within **3 months** post-surgery; LASIK-treated corneas take much longer to recover. ([Journal of Clinical Medicine / PMC, 2023](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10253504/))
- Chronic dry eye rate: 5.0% for PRK, 0.8% for LASIK (noting different patient selection factors). Most symptoms are transient and resolve within 12 months for both procedures. ([JCRS / PMC, 2012](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5702539/))

#### Corneal Biomechanical Stability
- TransPRK has **the strongest biostability** of any laser procedure (no flap created, no stromal weakening from cutting). ([Journal of Chinese Medical Association / PMC, 2022](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12755589/))
- Surface ablation **preserves more corneal stromal tissue** than LASIK, making it the preferred choice for thinner corneas, contact sport athletes, and military personnel. ([BMC Ophthalmology, 2020](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7304146/))

### Long-Term Outcomes (8–20 Years)
- A 2025 study tracking patients **8–10 years post-PRK** found:
  - Safety index: **1.0** (no loss of best-corrected vision)
  - Efficacy index: **0.95** (near-perfect)
  - Predictability index: **0.97**
  - **100% of eyes maintained 20/30 or better**; 72% at 20/20 at 10 years
  - No cases of corneal haze observed at 10-year follow-up
  ([Beyoglu Eye Journal / PMC, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12231249/))
- Follow-up data spanning **10–20 years** confirm surface ablation outcomes are "stable and safe over the long term." ([Greenwich Eye, 2026](https://greenwicheye.com/article/prk-vs-lasek-vs-epi-lasik-surface-ablation/))
- A 12-year follow-up study (ScienceDirect) found postoperative refraction remained stable with no significant change in mean spherical equivalent between 1, 6, and 12 years. ([ScienceDirect](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0161642004008309))
- A meta-analysis found no statistically significant differences in effectiveness between PRK, LASEK, Epi-LASIK, and LASIK when measured by uncorrected visual acuity — confirming surface ablation as a fully equivalent alternative. ([Greenwich Eye, 2026](https://greenwicheye.com/article/prk-vs-lasek-vs-epi-lasik-surface-ablation/))

## 6. Psychological & Quality of Life Impact

### Quality of Life Studies
- **Meta-analysis (2025, Journal of Ophthalmology), n=1,753 patients, 11 studies:** Laser vision correction significantly improved quality of life at 1, 3, and 6+ months post-surgery (SMD = 0.38 at 1 month → 1.03 at 3 months). Improvement is more pronounced in younger patients. ([PMC, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11730016/))
- **Post-LASIK QOL was statistically equivalent to emmetropes** (people with naturally perfect vision) — meaning surgery restores quality of life to baseline "perfect vision" levels. ([Journal of Ophthalmology, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11730016/))
- Validated questionnaires used across studies: NEI RQL-42, NEI VFQ-25, QIRC, SF-36 — all showing significant improvements post-surgery.

### Happiness Research
- A study from Minamiaoyama Eye Clinic, Japan (n=307 patients) found that **happiness scores measured on the Subjective Happiness Scale (SHS) rose significantly after LASIK surgery**. ([Waite Vision, 2025 citing PMC study](https://www.waitevision.com/lasik-utah/can-lasik-improve-happiness-what-the-science-says/))
- A published study in *Patient Preference and Adherence* confirmed **LASIK positively affected patients' overall subjective happiness postoperatively**, correlating with surgical satisfaction and improved QOL. ([PMC, 2018](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC6161728/))
- A Capella University study found that patients who had LASIK or PRK reported:
  - Higher self-esteem post-surgery
  - Reduced symptoms of depression and stress
  - More positive self-perception in both personal and professional life
  ([Elite LASIK & Cataract, 2025](https://2020lasikindianapolis.com/lasik-and-self-esteem/))
- The CDC reports **1 in 4 people experience anxiety and depression** related to their vision issues — laser correction addresses root cause. ([Brinton Vision, 2024](https://brintonvision.com/lasik-questions/can-lasik-improve-self-esteem-what-the-science-says/))

### Freedom & Independence
- Post-surgery patients consistently report:
  - Wake up with immediate, clear vision — no morning ritual
  - Engage in swimming, sports, travel without planning around visual correction
  - No dependence on external objects for basic function
  - Improved confidence in professional and social settings
- Elimination of glasses/lens inconveniences (dirty, scratched, lost, fogged, forgotten) directly linked to improved social roles, job performance, and intimate relationships. ([Journal of Ophthalmology / PMC, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11730016/))

### "Best Decision of My Life"
- "It was the best decision I ever made!" and "Best money I ever spent!" are described as **the two most common things people say after LASIK**. ([Waite Vision, 2025](https://www.waitevision.com/lasik-utah/can-lasik-improve-happiness-what-the-science-says/))
- RealSelf social media analysis (n=523 LASIK reviews): **76.7% of written reviews were positive**; refractive result was the #1 cited reason for satisfaction. ([Clinical Ophthalmology / PMC, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12219200/))

## 7. Interesting Facts & Hook Ideas

### World Population Stats
- **~4 billion people worldwide** wear prescription glasses — **57% of the global population**. That's more people wearing glasses than using smartphones. ([Glasson Optical Software, 2025](https://www.glasson.app/blog/what-percentage-of-people-wear-glasses-the-global-vision-correction-reality/))
- **Japan** leads the world with **73.9%** of its population wearing glasses. Myopia rates in East Asian cities approach **90%**. ([Glasson Optical Software, 2025](https://www.glasson.app/blog/what-percentage-of-people-wear-glasses-the-global-vision-correction-reality/))
- In the US: **75% of adults need vision correction**; **64% wear glasses** = ~197.6 million Americans. ([Glasson, 2025](https://www.glasson.app/blog/what-percentage-of-people-wear-glasses-the-global-vision-correction-reality/))
- **83–89%** of Americans over 45 need reading glasses due to presbyopia — almost universal by middle age. ([Glasson, 2025](https://www.glasson.app/blog/what-percentage-of-people-wear-glasses-the-global-vision-correction-reality/))
- Myopia has **tripled in children since 1990** — now affecting 1 in 3 kids globally. ([Glasson, 2025](https://www.glasson.app/blog/what-percentage-of-people-wear-glasses-the-global-vision-correction-reality/))

### Time Spent Cleaning
- Experts recommend cleaning glasses **daily** — yet **1 in 4 people clean theirs once a month or less**. ([NVISION Eye Centers, 2024](https://www.nvisioncenters.com/survey-americas-bad-eye-care-habits/))
- **36%** of people use their shirt or clothing to clean glasses — scratching lenses and defeating the purpose. ([Vision Council / Optical Prism, 2024](https://opticalprism.ca/new-research-reveals-more-than-one-third-of-eyewear-users-neglect-proper-care/))
- If a person cleans their glasses just 3 times per day (1 min each = 3 min/day), that's **18+ hours per year** spent on glasses maintenance. Over 20 years: **360+ hours** — 15 full days of life.

### Environmental Impact
- Americans alone flush enough contact lenses to fill **~2.7 million water bottles' worth of plastic** into wastewater annually.
- **19–21%** of contact lens wearers dispose of lenses by flushing them down the toilet or sink. ([Scientific American, 2018](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/contact-lenses-are-a-surprising-source-of-pollution/); [Statista](https://www.statista.com/chart/15181/environmental-impact-of-flushing-contact-lenses/))
- Contact lens plastic does not break down in wastewater treatment — it fragments into **microplastics** that enter food chains.
- Laser eye surgery = **zero ongoing plastic waste** from lenses, packaging, or solution bottles.

### Surprising / Counterintuitive Facts
1. **The US military prefers surface ablation (PRK/TransPRK) over LASIK** for combat personnel and pilots — not because it's "older" but because it's structurally *safer* (no flap to displace from blast, impact, or high G-forces). ([US Army Benefits, 2021](https://myarmybenefits.us.army.mil/News/Military-Laser-Eye-Surgery-Enhancing-Vision-Readiness))
2. **Ectasia (the most feared complication of laser surgery) is 4.5× more likely with LASIK than surface ablation.** Yet LASIK is marketed as the "premium" option. ([PMC / Ophthalmology & Therapy, 2021](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC8589911/))
3. **99% of contact lens wearers** are engaging in at least one behavior that puts them at risk for a potentially blinding eye infection — right now, today. ([CDC, 2015](https://www.cdc.gov/mmwr/preview/mmwrhtml/mm6432a2.htm))
4. **Laser surgery may be cheaper than contacts within 4–6 years** — yet most people perceive it as unaffordable. ([University of Utah Health, 2026](https://healthcare.utah.edu/healthfeed/2026/01/lasik-vs-cost-of-glasses-and-contacts-smart-investment-your-vision))
5. **Post-LASIK/PRK quality of life is statistically indistinguishable from people born with perfect vision** (emmetropes). ([Journal of Ophthalmology / PMC, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC11730016/))
6. **76.7% of LASIK reviews on social media are positive** — the procedure has stronger social satisfaction data than nearly any elective surgery. ([Clinical Ophthalmology / PMC, 2025](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC12219200/))
7. **Surface ablation (TransPRK/PRK) achieves better clinical outcomes vs. FS-LASIK** in randomized studies for high myopia correction, with higher predictability and fewer higher-order aberrations. ([BMC Ophthalmology, 2020](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC7304146/))

- **Emotional hook:** "Best decision I ever made" is the most-reported post-surgery sentiment — more resonant than clinical stats.
